"Sol Iustitiae et Occidentem Illustra"

Rutgers University, the State University of New Jersey, is a leading public research institution with campuses in New Brunswick, Newark, and Camden. As one of the oldest and most respected universities in the United States, Rutgers offers students a broad and diverse academic experience. With over 150 undergraduate majors and more than 400 graduate programs, students can pursue degrees in fields such as engineering, business, the humanities, social sciences, life sciences, health professions, and more. The university is also home to highly ranked professional schools, including the Rutgers Business School, the School of Engineering, and the Ernest Mario School of Pharmacy.

In addition to its academic offerings, Rutgers provides numerous opportunities for research, internships, global study, and community engagement. Students have access to cutting-edge research facilities and are encouraged to work alongside faculty on innovative projects across disciplines. The university also offers extensive continuing education and professional development programs for adult learners and working professionals. With a vibrant campus life that includes hundreds of student organizations, NCAA Division I athletics, and cultural resources like the Zimmerli Art Museum and Mason Gross School of the Arts, Rutgers fosters both personal and intellectual growth. Its commitment to public service, diversity, and global citizenship makes it a dynamic and inclusive environment for students from all backgrounds.

Students benefit from a strong research environment, world-class faculty, access to multiple libraries, and cultural institutions such as the Zimmerli Art Museum. Rutgers also emphasizes global learning and gives students chances to study abroad.
